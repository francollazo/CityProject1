Legends and history of Madrid

Madrid's origins date back to the pre-Roman period, when the area was no more than a rural region. The remains of a Visigothic basilica found on the present site of the Almudena Cathedral demonstrate the strong likelihood of settlement.

The first evidence of permanent settlement is during the Muslim period, with the construction of a defensive fortress by the river. It is believed that the name Madrid comes from this 9th century Muslim fortified settlement, called Mayrīţ.

It is thought to have been built very close to the present-day site of the Royal Palace, on a promontory by the Manzanares River.
