Monuments

This website contains information about the monuments and mythical places of Madrid.

In Madrid, there are many monuments that are very important for the country and which are also public and can be visited by everyone free of charge, such as the Retiro Park.

Plaza Mayor

Probably the most visited monument in Madrid and dates back to the 16th century. It was the main marketplace, the scene of events such as bullfights or public executions -by gallows, knife or axe-, and today it is one of the nerve centres of tourism. In the middle is the equestrian statue of Felipe III, from 1606, another Madrid icon.

Puerta de Alcalá

The Puerta de Alcalá, located in the Plaza de la Independencia, was commissioned by Madrid's favourite king, Carlos III. It was one of the five ancient royal gates that gave access to the city for travellers coming from France. It was the first triumphal arch built in Europe after the fall of the Roman Empire, before the Arc de Triomphe in Paris and the Brandenburg Gate in Berlin.

Retiro Park

Our little Central Park is one of the places you have to visit in Madrid. Every self-respecting Madrileño has enjoyed a Sunday boat ride on its pond. This PRECIOUS park is home to a lot of unforgettable artistic references: architectural, sculptural and landscape ensembles from the 17th to the 21st centuries and the famous Palacio de Cristal. For mystery lovers... it contains one of the few statues in the world dedicated to Lucifer, 'The Fountain of the Fallen Angel', at an exact height of 666 metres above sea level.
